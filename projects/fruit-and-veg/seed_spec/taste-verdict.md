# TASTE Verdict — Fruit & Veg

## Verdict: PASS
## Mode: HOLD
## Confidence: 0.9

## Sharpened Brief

**One-liner:** Fruit & Veg — Kids discover and collect fruit and vegetable knowledge through playful flashcards.

**Persona:** A 5-8 year old (and their parent/family member), who encounters unfamiliar food and wants to learn about it in a fun way.

**Problem:** Kids have no structured, delightful way to learn about fruit and vegetables — existing tools are either boring or not purpose-built.

**Killer edge:** Discovery-as-collection. Each fruit/veg is a character that talks to you. You collect knowledge like stickers. Learning IS the game mechanic.

**Mode applied:** Hold

**Scope boundaries:**
- **IN:** Card experience with first-person voice, multiple quiz types, sticker book collection, category badges, daily challenge (Fruit of the Day + 2 review), streak tracking, curated static catalogue (~60-80 items), optional accounts with progress sync, bright kid-friendly design
- **OUT:** AR mode, voice narration, multiplayer/social, physical goods, in-app purchases, ads, recipe connections
- **DEFERRED (v2+):** Secret discovery achievements, streak garden visualization, expanded catalogue (herbs/nuts/grains), seasonal content, classroom mode

**Vision alignment:** Ships the core product that all future expansions build on — the catalogue, the collection mechanic, and the daily habit loop.

## Analysis Notes

- Premise is sound: genuine gap in kid-friendly food education
- Persona is concrete and testable
- Killer edge (discovery-as-collection with personality) survives at minimum scope
- Daily challenge is load-bearing for retention — must stay in v1
- Accounts/sync justified by meta-goal (Rouge end-to-end testing)
- Secret discoveries and streak garden deferred without losing core value
- Natural evolution path to 12-month vision, no pivot needed
- Cost of inaction is low → product must earn attention through delight, which the design accounts for
