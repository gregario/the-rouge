# Product Hunt Launch Copy — Fruit & Veg

## TITLE
Fruit & Veg — Flashcard learning for kids, one fruit a day

## TAGLINE
Kids discover 75 fruits and vegetables through playful quizzes and sticker collecting

## DESCRIPTION

Most kids can't tell you where a banana grows or why a strawberry isn't really a berry. The tools that exist are either boring flashcard apps or games where learning is an afterthought. There's nothing that makes food knowledge genuinely fun for young children.

Fruit & Veg is a flashcard app where every fruit and vegetable talks to your child in its own voice. "I grow on tall trees in South America!" Kids flip the card, read fun facts, then answer simple quizzes — tap the right colour, guess where it grows, true or false. Every card completed earns a sticker for their collection.

What makes it stick is the daily challenge. Each morning there's a new Fruit of the Day plus 2 review cards. It's the Wordle mechanic applied to food education — a simple habit that brings kids back every day. No penalties for wrong answers, no lives, no fail states. Just colourful cards, gentle learning, and a sticker book that slowly fills with colour.

Try it now — no sign-up needed, completely free, no ads.

## MAKER COMMENT

I built this because my niece couldn't name five vegetables, and I realised there was literally nothing out there that made learning about food fun for a 6-year-old. Everything was either a dull quiz app or a game where the "education" was an afterthought.

I wanted something that felt like collecting Pokémon cards but actually taught her something real — where food comes from, how it grows, what makes each one interesting. The first-person voice was the breakthrough: instead of "bananas grow in tropical regions," the banana says "I grow on tall trees where it's really warm!" Same information, completely different experience for a kid.

It's free, it's ad-free, and it works without an account. If your kid likes stickers and likes knowing things, they'll like this.

Would love feedback — especially from parents and teachers.
