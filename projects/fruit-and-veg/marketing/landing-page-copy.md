## PROBLEM

- **Section headline:** Kids don't know where their food comes from
- **Body:** Ask a 6-year-old where bananas grow and you'll get a shrug. Most children can name a handful of fruits and vegetables, but they don't know what makes each one special — where it's from, how it grows, or why a strawberry isn't really a berry. The tools that exist are either dull flashcard apps or games where learning is an afterthought.

## SOLUTION

- **Section headline:** Meet your food — one card at a time
- **Body:** Every fruit and vegetable is a character that talks to your child. "I grow on tall trees in South America!" They flip the card, read fun facts, then answer simple questions to earn a sticker. 75 fruits and vegetables to discover, a new one every day. It's Wordle meets Pokémon — for the dinner table.

## FEATURES

### Daily Discovery
- **Description:** A new Fruit of the Day every morning, plus 2 review cards to keep knowledge fresh.
- **Detail:** The daily challenge creates a simple habit — "what's today's fruit?" — that brings kids back day after day.

### Playful Quizzes
- **Description:** 2-3 questions after every card — tap the right colour, guess where it grows, true or false.
- **Detail:** Multiple question types keep it fun. Wrong answers are met with a gentle "Nearly! I actually grow on trees!" — no penalties, no pressure.

### Sticker Collection
- **Description:** Every fruit learned earns a sticker. Fill your collection book with 75 colourful stickers.
- **Detail:** Kids see their progress as the grey silhouettes fill with colour. Complete a whole category — berries, tropical, root veg — to earn a special badge.

### Streaks & Achievements
- **Description:** Play every day and watch your streak grow. Category badges reward deep exploration.
- **Detail:** A gentle streak mechanic rewards consistency without punishing missed days. Your flower grows but never wilts.

### Save Progress
- **Description:** Create an optional account to save stickers across devices. Parent sets it up in 30 seconds.
- **Detail:** Works without an account — progress saves on the device. Add an account when your child has stickers worth keeping.

## FAQ

**Is this safe for my child?**
No ads, no chat, no social features, no tracking. We don't collect any information from children. If you create an account, only a parent's email is stored — and you can delete it at any time.

**What age is this for?**
Designed for 5-8 year olds (early readers). The questions use short sentences and big, tappable buttons. A confident 4-year-old can play with a parent reading aloud.

**How long does the content last?**
With 75 fruits and vegetables and a daily challenge format, there's roughly 3-4 months of new discoveries. After that, review mode keeps the knowledge fresh.

**Does my child need an account?**
No. The app works immediately with no sign-up. Progress saves on the device. An account is optional — it just lets you keep stickers if you switch devices.

**Can I use this in a classroom?**
The current version is designed for individual use. Classroom features (multi-student tracking, curriculum alignment) are on the roadmap.
