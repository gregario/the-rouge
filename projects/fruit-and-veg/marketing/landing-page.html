Kids don't know where their food comes from

Ask a 6-year-old where bananas grow and you'll get a shrug. Most children can name a handful of fruits and vegetables, but they don't know what makes each one special — where it's from, how it grows, or why a strawberry isn't really a berry. The tools that exist are either dull flashcard apps or games where learning is an afterthought.

Meet your food — one card at a time

Every fruit and vegetable is a character that talks to your child. "I grow on tall trees in South America!" They flip the card, read fun facts, then answer simple questions to earn a sticker. 75 fruits and vegetables to discover, a new one every day. It's Wordle meets Pokémon — for the dinner table.

How it works

Daily Discovery

A new Fruit of the Day every morning, plus 2 review cards to keep knowledge fresh.

The daily challenge creates a simple habit — "what's today's fruit?" — that brings kids back day after day.

Playful Quizzes

2-3 questions after every card — tap the right colour, guess where it grows, true or false.

Wrong answers are met with a gentle "Nearly! I actually grow on trees!" — no penalties, no pressure.

Sticker Collection

Every fruit learned earns a sticker. Fill your collection book with 75 colourful stickers.

Complete a whole category — berries, tropical, root veg — to earn a special badge.

Streaks & Achievements

Play every day and watch your streak grow. Category badges reward deep exploration.

A gentle streak mechanic rewards consistency without punishing missed days.

Save Progress

Optional account to save stickers across devices. Parent sets it up in 30 seconds.

Works without an account — progress saves on the device. Add an account when stickers are worth keeping.

Free for everyone

No ads. No in-app purchases. No premium tier. Create an optional account to sync progress across devices — also free.

Questions

Is this safe for my child?

No ads, no chat, no social features, no tracking. We don't collect any information from children. If you create an account, only a parent's email is stored — and you can delete it at any time.

What age is this for?

Designed for 5-8 year olds. Short sentences, big buttons. A confident 4-year-old can play with a parent reading aloud.

How long does the content last?

75 fruits and vegetables with a daily challenge format gives roughly 3-4 months of new discoveries. After that, review mode keeps the knowledge fresh.

Does my child need an account?

No. The app works immediately with no sign-up. Progress saves on the device. An account is optional — it just lets you keep stickers if you switch devices.

Can I use this in a classroom?

The current version is for individual use. Classroom features are on the roadmap.
